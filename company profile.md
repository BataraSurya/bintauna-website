\## 2. Core Positioning



bintauna.io should not present itself as a generic software house.



It should feel like:



\- a serious regional technology company

\- rooted in North Sulawesi

\- ambitious in scope

\- highly experimental but grounded in real implementation

\- capable of serving both public-sector and frontier-AI use cases

\- small, sharp, and builder-led

\*\*We build useful software from the edge, not the center.\*\*

\*\*We build products that connect local relevance with global technology.\*\*

\## 3. Brand Narrative



bintauna.io was born from a builder mindset: create real systems, test ideas quickly, and solve problems that matter. Based in Bintauna, in North Bolaang Mongondow, the company represents a different kind of technology story — one that does not begin in Jakarta, Singapore, San Francisco, or London, but from a coastal district in North Sulawesi.



This geographic reality should be treated as a strength, not a footnote.



bintauna.io stands for the idea that meaningful software can be designed and shipped from anywhere — including places the global tech narrative rarely pays attention to. The company’s projects reflect that range: public-sector digital systems, AI-assisted medical software, experimental creative tools, and infrastructure that lets language models interact with design environments and drawing systems.



The website should communicate confidence, competence, originality, and rootedness.

\## 12. Visual Direction



The website should feel like a premium modern studio site, but with regional identity and technical sharpness.



\### Design character

\- dark or deep neutral base with restrained accents

\- cinematic but clean

\- minimal clutter

\- typography-led

\- strong spacing

\- clear structure

\- subtle motion

\- project-first presentation

\- premium editorial feel, not SaaS dashboard feel



\### Visual themes to explore

\- coastal / map / topographic / spatial cues

\- subtle reference to North Sulawesi geography

\- understated technical diagrams or grid systems

\- elegant code-meets-atlas aesthetic

\- sense of place without becoming folkloric or decorative



\### Avoid

\- template-like startup layouts

\- generic stock illustrations

\- fake 3D blobs

\- excessive gradients everywhere

\- overdone neon cyberpunk

\- dashboard UI on the homepage unless it supports the narrative

\## 20. Technical Build Guidance for AI Agents



The site should be built as a premium company site, not a bloated CMS-first corporate template.



\### Recommended stack

\- Next.js

\- React

\- Tailwind CSS

\- Framer Motion for restrained motion

\- Static-first architecture unless CMS is truly needed

\- Good metadata and Open Graph support

\- Clean project-based content structure



\### Suggested content architecture

Use a structured content layer such as:

\- `company.ts`

\- `projects.ts`

\- `services.ts`

\- `contact.ts`



This makes it easy to maintain and later connect to a CMS if needed.



\### Accessibility and performance

\- strong typography contrast

\- semantic headings

\- responsive layout

\- optimized images

\- fast first load

\- no unnecessary animation overload
